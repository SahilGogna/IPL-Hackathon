# How Your Project Will Be Scored

---

## Phase 1 — Descriptive Analysis

Each project is scored across 5 dimensions for a maximum of 100 points.

---

**Depth of Analysis — 30 points**
Goes beyond surface-level stats.

- 25–30: Uncovers non-obvious patterns; layers multiple metrics to tell a richer story
- 15–24: Solid analysis with some depth; a few interesting findings beyond the basics
- 0–14: Mostly basic aggregations (totals, averages) with little exploration

---

**Quality of Visualizations — 25 points**
Charts are the right type for the data, clearly labelled, and easy to read.

- 21–25: Right chart type, well-labelled, clean, titles explain the finding not just the metric
- 13–20: Mostly clear with minor issues (missing labels, chart type mismatch)
- 0–12: Confusing, cluttered, or misleading charts

---

**Narrative & Storytelling — 20 points**
The project has a central question or theme and findings build on each other logically.

- 17–20: Clear through-line; someone outside the team can follow the story
- 10–16: Some structure but findings feel loosely connected
- 0–9: A collection of charts with no clear narrative thread

---

**Accuracy & Integrity — 15 points**
Numbers are verifiable, methodology is transparent, no misleading framing.

- 13–15: Metrics clearly defined, scope stated, sample sizes acknowledged where relevant
- 8–12: Mostly accurate with minor ambiguities
- 0–7: Errors in calculations or findings are cherry-picked without disclosure

---

**Creativity & Originality — 10 points**
A fresh angle or question — something you wouldn't find on a standard cricket stats site.

- 9–10: Genuinely novel insight or framing that makes use of the ball-by-ball depth
- 5–8: Some original thinking even if the core analysis is familiar
- 0–4: Covers well-trodden ground with no new perspective

---

## Phase 2 — Predictive Analysis

Each project is scored across 5 dimensions for a maximum of 100 points.

---

**Model Performance — 30 points**
How well does the model actually perform on unseen data?

- 25–30: Strong performance on holdout/test data; appropriate metric used for the problem type
- 15–24: Reasonable performance with honest evaluation
- 0–14: Poor performance, overfitting, or evaluation done only on training data

---

**Feature Engineering — 25 points**
Did you extract meaningful signals from the raw ball-by-ball data?

- 21–25: Features are thoughtful, cricket-context-aware, and clearly justified
- 13–20: Decent feature set with some domain thinking applied
- 0–12: Raw fields used as-is with no domain-driven transformation

---

**Methodology & Rigour — 20 points**
Is the approach sound and reproducible?

- 17–20: Clear train/test split or cross-validation, no data leakage, methodology documented
- 10–16: Mostly sound with minor gaps in documentation or validation approach
- 0–9: No proper validation, data leakage present, or methodology unclear

---

**Presentation of Results — 15 points**
Are the model's outputs clearly communicated — not just to data scientists, but to anyone?

- 13–15: Results explained intuitively; error analysis included; limitations acknowledged
- 8–12: Results shown but limited context or interpretation
- 0–7: Raw numbers dumped with no explanation of what they mean

---

**Creativity & Problem Framing — 10 points**
Did the team ask an interesting predictive question?

- 9–10: Problem is well-scoped, meaningful, and non-trivial (e.g. not just "predict match winner")
- 5–8: Reasonable problem with some original thinking in the approach
- 0–4: Generic problem framing with no distinguishing angle
