## Phase 1 — Descriptive Analysis

**What is descriptive analysis?**

Descriptive analysis means using data to understand and explain what has already happened. You are not predicting anything — you are finding patterns, trends, and insights that tell a story about the past. Think of it as answering the question: *what does this data actually tell us?*

**Some ideas to spark your thinking:**

- Which teams have the best powerplay record over 18 seasons?
- How has the role of the toss changed across seasons?
- Which bowlers are most economical in the death overs?
- Which venues favour batters vs bowlers?
- How do partnerships break down across batting positions?

These are just starting points. The best submissions will find angles nobody else thought of.

**What to submit:**

- A GitHub repository with clean, well-documented code
- At least one visualization or dashboard
- A short writeup (in your README or a separate document) explaining your key finding in plain language
- *(Good to have)* A short Loom video walking through your visualizations and key insights
